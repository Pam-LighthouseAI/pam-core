## STATEMENT OF NEED

### The Problem

In Ottawa, thousands of young people face barriers that limit their potential:

| Challenge | Ottawa Data | Source |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Youth in low-income households | 23% | Statistics Canada, 2021 |
| High school non-completion | 12% | OCSB, 2023 |
| Youth unemployment | 14% | StatsCan LFS, 2024 |
| Waitlist for youth mental health | 6-12 months | CHEO, 2023 |

### Who is Affected

Our target population is youth ages 12-18 in Ottawa's priority neighborhoods who face one or more risk factors:
- Living in low-income households
- Single-parent or no-parent families
- Struggling academically
- Limited access to positive role models
- Social isolation

### The Gap

While several mentoring programs exist in Ottawa, significant gaps remain:
- Current programs have waitlists of 50+ youth
- Few programs serve the critical 12-18 age range
- Limited programming in Nepean and Gloucester
- No programs specifically for youth with incarcerated parents

### Community Voice

> "Before my mentor, I didn't think anyone cared. Now I have someone who checks on me every week and helps me think about my future."
> — Marcus, 16, program participant

> "As a single parent working two jobs, I can't always be there. My son's mentor fills a gap I can't."
> — Parent of participant

### Why Now

The COVID-19 pandemic exacerbated youth mental health challenges and social isolation. Youth-serving organizations report increased demand, and schools identify more students needing support. Now is the time to invest in proven interventions.

## PROJECT DESCRIPTION

### Overview

The Pathways Youth Mentoring Program matches at-risk youth ages 12-18 with trained volunteer mentors for a minimum of one year. Mentors commit to meeting their mentee 2-4 hours per week, building a consistent, supportive relationship.

### Goals and Objectives

**Goal:** At-risk youth develop the skills, confidence, and support they need to reach their potential.

**Objective 1:** By March 2027, 100 youth will be matched with trained mentors, exceeding our current capacity by 67%.

**Objective 2:** By the end of Year 2, 85% of youth participants will demonstrate improved academic engagement as measured by school attendance and grades.

**Objective 3:** By the end of Year 2, 90% of youth will report having a trusted adult in their lives, as measured by pre/post surveys.

### Activities

| Activity | Timeline | Who | Outputs |
