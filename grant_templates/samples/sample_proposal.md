## 2. Executive Summary

```
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Organization: Ottawa Youth Services
Project Title: Youth Empowerment After-School Program
Funding Request: $75,000
Project Duration: September 2026 - August 2027

ORGANIZATION OVERVIEW
Ottawa Youth Services is a registered charity that has served Ottawa's 
youth since 2001. Our mission is to empower young people to reach their 
full potential through mentorship, education, and support. We serve 
approximately 1,200 youth annually through five core programs.

PROJECT SUMMARY
The Youth Empowerment After-School Program provides free programming 
for at-risk youth ages 12-18 during the critical 3pm-6pm hours. This 
existing program offers academic support, life skills workshops, 
recreational activities, and mentorship.

TARGET POPULATION
We will serve 150 youth ages 12-18 from low-income families in the 
Vanier, Overbrook, and Heatherington neighborhoods of Ottawa.

KEY OUTCOMES
- 80% of participants will improve or maintain academic performance
- 90% of participants will report increased sense of belonging
- 70% of participants will demonstrate improved life skills

PARTNERSHIPS
- Ottawa-Carleton District School Board - referrals and space
- Big Brothers Big Sisters Ottawa - mentor recruitment

SUSTAINABILITY
After United Way funding, this project will be sustained through 
municipal grants, foundation funding, and individual donations.
```

## 3. Statement of Need

```
STATEMENT OF NEED

THE PROBLEM
Youth in low-income Ottawa neighborhoods face significant barriers to 
success. The hours between 3pm and 6pm are critical - this is when 
unsupervised youth are most likely to engage in risky behaviors, 
including substance use, criminal activity, and unhealthy relationships.

COMMUNITY DATA
According to Statistics Canada (2021):
- 18.3% of children in Ottawa live in low-income households
- Youth crime rates peak between 3pm-6pm on weekdays
- 35% of Ottawa youth report feeling lonely or isolated

In Vanier, Overbrook, and Heatherington:
- Poverty rates exceed 25% (Ottawa Neighbourhood Study, 2023)
- High school graduation rates are 15% below city average
- Wait lists for existing youth programs exceed 200 names

ROOT CAUSES
1. Economic disadvantage limiting access to quality programming
2. Parent work schedules creating supervision gaps
3. Social isolation and lack of positive peer networks
4. Limited transportation options in underserved neighborhoods

CURRENT GAPS
While some after-school programs exist, they face barriers:
- Cost: Average $200-400/month per youth
- Location: Concentrated in wealthier areas
- Capacity: Long wait lists at free programs

HOW WE ADDRESS THE NEED
The Youth Empowerment Program removes barriers by:
- Offering completely free programming
- Operating in three neighborhood locations
- Providing transportation assistance
- Serving 150 youth with no wait list

ALIGNMENT WITH UNITED WAY PRIORITIES
This project directly supports "All That Kids Can Be" by ensuring 
vulnerable youth have the support they need to succeed academically, 
socially, and emotionally.
```
