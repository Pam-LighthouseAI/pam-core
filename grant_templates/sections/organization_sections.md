## 3. GOALS & OBJECTIVES TEMPLATES

### SMART Objectives Format

**Goal:** [Broad outcome statement]

**Objective 1:** By [date], [number] [participants] will [action] as measured by [method], increasing from [baseline] to [target].

**Objective 2:** Within [timeframe], [percentage]% of [participants] will demonstrate [outcome] as shown by [evidence].

**Objective 3:** By [date], [indicator] will improve by [percentage/number], from [baseline] to [target].

## 4. EVALUATION SECTIONS

### Evaluation Framework

> [Organization Name] uses a mixed-methods evaluation approach to measure both quantitative outputs and qualitative outcomes. Our evaluation plan includes:

**Process Evaluation:**
- Tracking participation numbers and demographics
- Monitoring program delivery fidelity
- Gathering participant feedback

**Outcome Evaluation:**
- Pre/post assessments using validated instruments
- Follow-up surveys at 3 and 6 months post-program
- Case studies and success stories

**Data Collection Methods:**

| Method | Purpose | Frequency |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Registration forms | Demographics, baseline | Intake |
| Attendance records | Participation tracking | Each session |
| Pre/post surveys | Knowledge, attitude change | Start/end |
| Focus groups | In-depth feedback | Midpoint, end |
| Follow-up surveys | Sustained change | 3, 6 months |

---

### Sample Indicators by Outcome Type

**Knowledge:**
- % of participants who can identify [key concept]
- Average score on [knowledge assessment]
- Self-reported understanding (pre/post)

**Skills:**
- % demonstrating [specific skill]
- Performance on [skills assessment]
- Self-efficacy ratings

**Behavior:**
- % reporting [behavior change]
- Frequency of [behavior] (pre/post)
- Maintenance at follow-up

**Conditions:**
- % with improved [condition]
- Change in [specific measure]
- Comparison to control/baseline
